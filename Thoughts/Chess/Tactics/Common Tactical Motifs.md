# ⚔️ Common Tactical Motifs

Tactics are short-term sequences of moves that limit the opponent's options and result in tangible gain (material or mate). As the saying goes, *"Chess is 99% tactics."*

## Basic Motifs

### 🍴 The Fork
A single piece attacks two or more of the opponent's pieces simultaneously.
*   **Key Piece:** Knights are the most famous forkers, but Pawns and Queens are also deadly.
*   **Goal:** Win material because the opponent can only save one piece.

### 📌 The Pin
A piece is held in place because moving it would expose a more valuable piece behind it.
*   **Absolute Pin:** The piece *cannot* move because it screens the King (illegal move).
*   **Relative Pin:** The piece *should not* move because it screens a Queen or Rook.
*   **Motto:** "Pin and win!"

### 🍢 The Skewer
Similar to a pin, but the valuable piece is in front. The valuable piece is forced to move, exposing the weaker target behind it.
*   **Example:** A Rook checks the King, the King moves, and the Rook captures the Queen behind the King.

## Intermediate Motifs

### 🕵️ Discovered Attack
One piece moves out of the way, revealing an attack from a piece behind it.
*   **Discovered Check:** The revealed attack is a check. This is incredibly dangerous because the moving piece can land anywhere (even en prise) since the opponent must address the check.

### 💨 Double Check
A specific type of discovered check where *both* the moving piece and the revealed piece give check simultaneously.
*   **Power:** The King *must* move. It cannot block or capture, because two pieces are attacking at once.

### 🧱 Overloading (Deflection)
A defensive piece is assigned too many tasks (e.g., protecting a Knight and a back-rank mate).
*   **Goal:** Distract the defender from one task to exploit the other weakness.

### 🥅 Removing the Defender
Simply capturing or forcing away a critical defensive piece to leave a target undefended.

### ☠️ Back Rank Weakness
When the King is trapped behind its own wall of pawns (usually h7/g7/f7) and a Rook or Queen lands on the back rank for checkmate.
*   **Prevention:** Play "Luft" (air) by pushing h3 or h6.

## Advanced Patterns

### 🌪️ The Windmill
A devastating series of discovered checks and captures.
*   **Famous Example:** *Torre vs. Lasker*. A Bishop checks, the King moves, the Rook discovers check, King moves back, Bishop takes another piece, check... repeat.

### 🧘 Zwissenzyg (Intermediate Move)
Inserting a surprising move (often a check or threat) in the middle of an expected sequence to change the outcome.
