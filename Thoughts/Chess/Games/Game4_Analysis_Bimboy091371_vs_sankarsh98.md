## Patterns to Avoid

1. **Knights on the Rim:** "Knight on the rim is dim" - avoid Nh6/Na6 unless there's a good reason
2. **Pointless Checks:** Don't give check just because you can - make sure it accomplishes something
3. **Material Down:** When behind material, avoid trades unless they help you

## Key Lessons

1. **Opening Principles:**
   - Develop pieces to natural squares (knights to f6/c6, not h6/a6)
   - Don't put pieces where they can be easily attacked
   - Knights belong in the center or near the center

2. **When Behind:**
   - Create complications and tactics
   - Don't simplify into endgames
   - Look for counterplay

3. **Calculation:**
   - Before making unusual moves (like Nh6), check if pieces can be attacked
   - Calculate forcing moves from opponent
