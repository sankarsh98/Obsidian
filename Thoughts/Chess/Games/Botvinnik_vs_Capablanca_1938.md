# ♟️ Botvinnik vs. Capablanca (1938)

**Opponent:** `Jose Raul Capablanca`
**Context:** AVRO Tournament. A clash of generations. Botvinnik, the Soviet rising star, vs. the old legend Capablanca.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 30. Ba3!**
One of the most famous deflection sacrifices in history. Botvinnik sacrifices the Bishop to distract the Black Queen from defending the e8 square, allowing his passed e-pawn to promote.
> "This game is the gold standard for attacking with a central pawn majority."

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Central Majority:** Botvinnik used his pawns on c4, d4, e4 to slowly roll forward, restricting Capablanca's pieces.
2.  **The e-Pawn Wedge:** The pawn on e6 was a "bone in the throat" for Black, splitting his army in two.
3.  **Tactics flow from Strategy:** The brilliant `Ba3!` was only possible because of the crushing positional advantage built over 29 moves.
