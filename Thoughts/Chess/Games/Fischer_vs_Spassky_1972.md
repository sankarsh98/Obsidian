# ♟️ Fischer vs. Spassky (1972) - Game 6

**Opponent:** `Boris Spassky (World Champion)`
**Context:** World Championship Match, Reykjavik. Fischer opens with 1. c4 (transposing to QGD), shocking Spassky who expected 1. e4.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 20. e4!**
Fischer strikes in the center to open lines for his bishops. Spassky's "Hanging Pawns" (c5 and d5) become targets rather than strengths.
> "The game is a symphony of positional play." - *Annotators*

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Surprise Value:** Fischer played the QGD (usually 1. d4) instead of his trademark 1. e4, throwing Spassky off his preparation.
2.  **Hanging Pawns:** Spassky accepted "Hanging Pawns" (c5/d5). Fischer showed how to restrict them and then attack the center with e4!.
3.  **The Exchange Sacrifice:** Fischer plays `Rxf6` later to ruin Black's King structure, valuing positional dominance over material.
