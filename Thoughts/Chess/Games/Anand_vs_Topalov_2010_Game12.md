# ♟️ Anand vs. Topalov (2010) - Game 12

**Opponent:** `Veselin Topalov`
**Context:** World Championship, Sofia. The final game of the match. Score is tied. Topalov (Black) plays aggressively to avoid rapid tiebreaks but pushes too hard.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 30... f5?**
Topalov lashes out with his King exposed. He tried to complicate the position but only created weaknesses that Anand ruthlessly exploited.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Solid Defense (Lasker):** The Lasker defense is incredibly solid. Anand had trouble breaking it until Topalov "committed suicide" by attacking prematurely.
2.  **King Safety:** In QGD positions, the center often opens late. Topalov forgot his King was unsafe when he opened the e-file.
3.  **Nerves:** This game proves that even Super GMs crack under pressure. Topalov avoided a drawish line and lost the title because of it.
