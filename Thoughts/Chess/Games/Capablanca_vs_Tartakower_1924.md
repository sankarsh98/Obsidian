# ♟️ Capablanca vs. Tartakower (1924)

**Opponent:** `Savielly Tartakower`
**Context:** New York 1924. Perhaps the most famous Rook Endgame in history.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 52. Kg4!**
The King enters the fray. Capablanca demonstrates that in Rook endgames, the King is an attacking piece. He shields his passed pawn and walks the King all the way to support promotion.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Rook Activity:** "Rooks belong behind passed pawns." Capablanca followed this golden rule perfectly.
2.  **Cutting off the King:** Capablanca used his Rook to cut the Black King off from the defense (the 6th rank).
3.  **Active Defense:** Tartakower defended passively. Modern theory says you must attack pawns in Rook endings, even if you are down material (Active Defense).
