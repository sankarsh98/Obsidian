## Patterns to Recognize

1. **Exchange Down Endgames:** When down the exchange (rook vs bishop), avoid further simplification unless you can create immediate threats
2. **Piece Coordination:** Black's rook and bishop worked together excellently in the endgame
3. **King Activity:** In endgames, the king must be active. Your king became too passive after move 30

## Key Lessons

1. **Tactical Awareness:** Always check for back-rank tactics and loose pieces before moving the queen away from defensive duties
2. **Material Down Strategy:** When behind in material:
   - Create complications and tactics
   - Don't trade pieces unless it improves your position
   - Look for perpetual check or fortress opportunities
3. **Defensive Technique:** After losing the exchange on move 18, should have tried to:
   - Keep queens on the board for counterplay
   - Create threats against Black's king
   - Avoid further simplification
