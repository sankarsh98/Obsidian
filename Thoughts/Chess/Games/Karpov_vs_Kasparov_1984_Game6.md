# ♟️ Karpov vs. Kasparov (1984) - Game 6

**Opponent:** `Garry Kasparov`
**Context:** World Championship Match. Karpov (White) delivers a masterclass in squeezing the opponent without taking risks.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 38. Rd5!**
Karpov cements a Rook in the center. It cannot be taken because of the weak back rank and potential hanging pawns. This paralyzed Kasparov's forces.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Opposite Colored Bishops:** Usually drawn, but here Karpov showed that with Rooks on the board, they favor the attacker (he controlled light squares, Kasparov couldn't defend them).
2.  **Accumulation of Advantages:** No single move won the game; it was 40 moves of improving piece position by millimeters.
3.  **Prophylaxis:** Karpov prevented every single active plan Kasparov attempted before it even started.
