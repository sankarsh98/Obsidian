# ♟️ Deep Blue vs. Kasparov (1997) - Game 6

**Opponent:** `Garry Kasparov`
**Context:** The "Rematch of the Century." The first time a computer defeated a reigning World Champion in a match. This was the final, decisive game.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 19. c4!**
Deep Blue plays a move Kasparov thought "impossible" for a machine. It offered a material sacrifice to gain a crushing positional bind. Kasparov, psychologically broken, collapsed quickly.
*(Note: Kasparov actually blundered in the opening with ...h6, inviting the sacrifice).*

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **The Knight Sacrifice (Nxe6):** A thematic Caro-Kann sacrifice. White destroys Black's pawn cover on e6/f7, leaving the King exposed in the center.
2.  **Psychology:** Kasparov played the Caro-Kann to be "solid," but Deep Blue played incredibly aggressively. You cannot play passively against accurate calculation.
3.  **Space:** White's space advantage in the Steinitz variation (1. e4 c6 2. d4 d5 3. Nc3 dxe4 4. Nxe4 Nd7 5. Ng5!) is dangerous if Black doesn't develop perfectly.
