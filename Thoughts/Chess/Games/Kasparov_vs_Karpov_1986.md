# ♟️ Kasparov vs. Karpov (1986) - Game 22
## *The Ultimate K vs K Battle*

**Event:** World Championship Rematch (Game 22)
**Location:** London/Leningrad
**Date:** October 3, 1986

---

## 📜 Historical Context

The Kasparov-Karpov rivalry is the greatest in chess history. Between 1984 and 1990, they played five World Championship matches—144 games in total. This game is from their third match in 1986.

Kasparov needed to win to take a commanding lead. What followed was a spectacular attacking display in the Queen's Gambit Declined, featuring perfect positional play followed by a brutal tactical finish.

**Match Score Before This Game:** Kasparov 10.5 - Karpov 10

---

## 🏆 Why This Game Matters

1. **The Greatest Rivalry:** Kasparov vs. Karpov defined an era. This game encapsulates their stylistic clash—Kasparov's dynamism vs. Karpov's tenacity.
2. **The Time Scramble:** Moves 30-40 were played in severe time trouble, yet both players found brilliant moves under pressure.
3. **The Knockout Blow:** Kasparov's combination at the end (Ne6+! Nd7+!) was worthy of Tal.

## 📉 The Critical Moment

### Move 40. Ne6+!

After a chaotic time scramble, Kasparov finds a stunning Knight sacrifice that forces either immediate checkmate or decisive material loss.

**The punch line:**
After 40. Ne6+! fxe6 41. **Nd7+!** — a double attack. If 41... Rxd7, then 42. Qxd8# is checkmate! Karpov must give up his Queen.

---

## 🎯 Strategic Themes

### 1. Space Advantage in the QGD
Kasparov used the typical QGD plan: build up on the Kingside with piece placement and small pawn moves (h4!, h5) to create a squeeze.

### 2. The e5 Outpost
The e5 square became a staging post for White's pieces. Once a piece lands on e5 in the QGD, Black's position is often under severe pressure.

### 3. Attacking Under Time Pressure
Both players had less than a minute for moves 30-40. Kasparov's ability to maintain tactical accuracy while Karpov struggled shows the importance of intuition and pattern recognition.

### 4. Knight Domination
The final combination features Knights at their best—jumping into the heart of the enemy camp with devastating effect.

---

## 🚦 Key Takeaways

1. **Use Your Space:** In the QGD, White often gets more space. Use it to build up pressure gradually before breaking through.

2. **The Time Scramble:** Practice blitz and bullet to improve tactical accuracy under pressure. Kasparov was feared for his time trouble play.

3. **Knight Sacrifices:** When Knights land deep in enemy territory (e6, d7), look for tactics! These "octopus" positions often lead to winning combinations.

4. **Prophylaxis AND Attack:** Kasparov didn't just attack—he prevented Karpov's counterplay while building his position.
