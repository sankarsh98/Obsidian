# ♟️ Kasparov vs. Karpov (1986) - Game 22

**Opponent:** `Anatoly Karpov`
**Context:** World Championship Rematch, London/Leningrad. A critical game where Kasparov needed a win to pull away.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 41. Nd7!**
After a chaotic time scramble before the 40th move, the dust settled. Kasparov found a beautiful Knight jump that dominates the board, cutting off the Black King's escape.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Space Advantage:** Kasparov used the QGD structure to gain massive space on the Kingside, forcing Karpov's pieces backward.
2.  **Prophylaxis vs Aggression:** Karpov (defense) tried to hold, but Kasparov (attack) kept finding small pawn pushes (h5, f5) to open lines.
3.  **The Time Trouble Scramble:** This game is famous for the moves 30-40 where both players were playing instantly, yet Kasparov maintained tactical accuracy.
