# ♟️ Lasker vs. Capablanca (1921) - Game 10

**Opponent:** `Emanuel Lasker`
**Context:** World Championship Match, Havana. This game marked the "changing of the guard" as Capablanca ground down the longtime champion.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 43... Rb1!**
Capablanca transitions into a Rook endgame that looks equal to a novice, but he calculated that his active King and better pawn structure would eventually force a win.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **The "Capablanca Endgame":** Do not fear simplification. Capablanca traded pieces willingly because he knew the resulting endgame was winning.
2.  **Pawn Structure:** White's isolated d-pawn (from the opening) became a long-term weakness that tied Lasker's pieces down.
3.  **Patience:** Capablanca maneuvered for over 20 moves without changing the pawn structure, just waiting for Lasker to make a tiny inaccuracy.
