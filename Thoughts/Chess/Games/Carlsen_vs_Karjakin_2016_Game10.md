# ♟️ Carlsen vs. Karjakin (2016) - Game 10

**Opponent:** `Sergey Karjakin`
**Context:** World Championship, NYC. Carlsen was trailing in the match and desperate for a win. This is a "grind" masterpiece.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 56... Nh7?**
Karjakin, the "Minister of Defense," finally cracked under 6+ hours of pressure. He missed a forced draw and allowed Carlsen's King to penetrate.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Creating Weaknesses:** Carlsen probed both sides of the board (Kingside and Queenside) until Karjakin's defense was stretched too thin.
2.  **Maneuvering:** Similar to Lasker vs Capablanca, Carlsen refused to draw, playing on even in a "dead drawn" rook endgame.
3.  **Psychological Pressure:** The QGD/Berlin structures are solid, but playing defense for 70 moves is exhausting. Carlsen bet on Karjakin's fatigue.
