# ♟️ Tal vs. Botvinnik (1961) - Game 8

**Opponent:** `Mikhail Botvinnik`
**Context:** World Championship Rematch. Tal (White) lost his title back to Botvinnik this match, but this game was a flash of his brilliance against the solid Caro-Kann.

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 25. e6!**
Tal rips open the e-file. A classic theme: when the opponent has a solid pawn structure, you must sacrifice to open lines (`e6`, `f5`, etc.).

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Attacking the Uncastled King:** Botvinnik delayed castling to hold the center. Tal punished this by sacrificing pawns to open the center files.
2.  **Activity over Material:** Tal didn't care about pawns; he cared about piece squares.
3.  **The "Slow" Caro-Kann:** This game shows the danger of the Caro-Kann. If you play too slowly, White can build a massive attack before you finish development.
