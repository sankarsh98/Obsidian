# ♟️ Fischer vs. Petrosian (1970)

**Opponent:** `Tigran Petrosian`
**Context:** USSR vs. Rest of the World. Fischer dismantles the greatest defender in history using the Caro-Kann!

## 📉 The Critical Moment
**Move 18. f5!**
Fischer launches a flank attack. Petrosian had a solid "fortress" on the queenside, so Fischer ignored it and smashed through on the Kingside.

## 🚦 Three Key Takeaways
1.  **Exchange Sacrifices:** Fischer sacrificed the Exchange (Rook for Knight) to eliminate Petrosian's best defender (the light-squared Bishop).
2.  **Weak Squares:** By removing the Bishop, Fischer conquered the light squares (d6, e5, f6).
3.  **Universal Style:** Fischer showed he could out-position even Petrosian. He didn't just calculate; he understood the *harmony* of the pieces.
