## The Moves
**1. e4 d5**

## The Concept
The Scandinavian Defense (or Center Counter Defense) is a provocative opening. Black immediately challenges White's control of the center by attacking the e4 pawn. After 2. exd5, Black usually recaptures with the Queen (2... Qxd5) or the Knight (2... Nf6). It forces the game into open lines immediately and eliminates White's dangerous e-pawn. While it loses time (moving the Queen early), it creates a solid, compact structure for Black similar to the Caro-Kann.

## Main Variations
*   **Mieses-Kotroc Variation (Main Line):** `2. exd5 Qxd5 3. Nc3 Qa5`
    *   *Description:* The Queen retreats to a safe square (a5). Black develops comfortably and puts pressure on the queenside.
*   **Modern Variation:** `2. exd5 Nf6`
    *   *Description:* Black delays recapturing the pawn to avoid bringing the Queen out early. Black will recapture with the Knight later (Nxd5).
*   **The Portuguese Gambit:** `2. exd5 Nf6 3. d4 Bg4`
    *   *Description:* A sharp gambit where Black offers a pawn to develop rapidly and attack White's center.

## Pros & Cons

| Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Simplicity:** Very easy to learn; forces White out of prepared e4 lines immediately. | **Tempo Loss:** In Queen lines, White gains time by attacking the Queen (Nc3). |
| **Solid Structure:** Black usually gets a "Caro-Kann" like structure (c6, e6) which is very tough. | **Space:** White usually controls more space in the center. |
| **Open Game:** Ensures an open game with pieces moving freely, unlike the French. | **Queen Exposure:** The Queen can become a target if Black isn't careful. |

## Key Ideas
*   **The Light-Squared Bishop:** Unlike the French Defense, Black's light-squared bishop is free to develop to f5 or g4 before playing e6.
*   **c6 and e6:** Black almost always builds a "small center" with pawns on c6 and e6 to blunt White's bishops.
*   **Pressure on d4:** White often has a d4 pawn that Black tries to encircle and attack.
