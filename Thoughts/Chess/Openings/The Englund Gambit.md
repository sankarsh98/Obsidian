# The Englund Gambit

## The Moves
**1. d4 e5?!**

## The Concept

The Englund Gambit is one of the most audacious (some say foolish) responses to 1.d4. Black immediately sacrifices a pawn to:
- Avoid boring positional chess
- Create immediate tactical chaos
- Exploit White's unfamiliarity
- Get rapid piece development

> ⚠️ **Objectivity Warning:** The Englund Gambit is **not objectively sound**. With correct play, White emerges with a clear advantage. However, at club level, White's unfamiliarity often leads to disasters.

**When to Play It:**
- Blitz/bullet games where time pressure matters
- Against opponents who play d4 for "quiet chess"
- When you need to win at any cost
- For pure entertainment value

---

# Critical Decision Point

After **1. d4 e5**, White has three main responses:

| Move | Evaluation | Character |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **2. dxe5** | Best, +0.5 to +1.0 | White takes the pawn (main line) |
| **2. e4** | Good, +0.3 | Transposes to other openings |
| **2. d5** | Playable | Avoids complications |
| **2. Nf3** | Safe | Declines the gambit |
| **2. e3?!** | Passive | Gives Black easy equality |

---

# PART 1: MAIN LINE (2. dxe5)

## The Mainline Tabiya

```
1. d4 e5 2. dxe5 Nc6
```

Black develops with tempo-like pressure on e5. Now White must decide how to defend.

---

## Variation 1: The Soller Gambit (3. Nf3 Qe7)

### The Refuted Trap Line

```
1. d4 e5 2. dxe5 Nc6 3. Nf3 Qe7 4. Bf4 Qb4+ 5. Bd2 Qxb2
```

**This is Black's "hope chess" line.** Black grabs b2, threatening Ra1.

### White's Best Response

```
6. Bc3! Bb4 7. Qd2!
```

**The Double Attack!** White attacks b4 and threatens Bxb4+.

After **7...Bxc3 8. Qxc3 Qxa1 9. Qxg7!** — White has a crushing attack.

```
Position after 9. Qxg7:
r . b . k . n r
p p p p . . Q p
. . n . . . . .
. . . . P . . .
. . . . . . . .
. . . . . N . .
P . P . P P P P
q N . . K B . R
```

[♟️ View this position on the board](https://lichess.org/editor/r1b1k1nr/pppp2Qp/2n5/4P3/8/5N2/P1P1PPPP/qN2KB1R_b_Kkq_-_0_9)

**Evaluation:** White is completely winning. Black's Queen is trapped on a1, and White threatens Qxh8, Qxf7+, and more.

> ❌ **Don't play this line!** Tempting, but ultimately losing with correct White play.

---

## Variation 2: The Correct Englund (3. Nf3 Qe7)

### The Zilbermints Gambit

```
1. d4 e5 2. dxe5 Nc6 3. Nf3 Qe7 4. Qd5!?
```

White's best response. Defends e5 and centralizes the Queen.

**Black's Best Continuation:**

```
4...f6 5. exf6 Nxf6 6. Qd1 d5
```

**Evaluation:** Black has some compensation — central pawns, developed pieces, open lines. But objectively, White is slightly better (~+0.5).

---

### The Hartlaub-Charlick Gambit

```
1. d4 e5 2. dxe5 d6
```

**The original Englund idea.** Black immediately pressures e5.

```
3. exd6 Bxd6 4. Nf3 Nf6 5. Bg5 h6 6. Bh4 g5!?
```

**Black claims:**
- The Bishop pair
- Open d-file for the Rook
- Active piece play

**Evaluation:** White is still better with ~+0.7, but Black has real practical chances.

---

## Variation 3: The Queen Trap Line (The Famous Trap!)

### Setup

```
1. d4 e5 2. dxe5 Nc6 3. Nf3 Qe7 4. Bf4 Qb4+ 5. Bd2 Qxb2 6. Nc3??
```

**White blunders!** This is what Black is hoping for.

```
6...Bb4! 7. Rb1 Qxc3! 8. Bxc3 Bxc3+
```

**Black wins the exchange and has a much better position.**

> ⭐ **The Dream:** This trap is WHY people play the Englund. When it works, it's beautiful. When White knows 6.Bc3!, you suffer.

# PART 2: ANTI-ENGLUND LINES

## White Declines: 2. Nf3

```
1. d4 e5 2. Nf3 e4 3. Nd2
```

White simply develops and waits for Black to overextend.

**Black's Best:**
```
3...d5 4. c3 Nf6 5. e3
```

**Evaluation:** Equal, but Black has achieved nothing special. The gambit spirit is lost.

---

## White Plays 2. e4

```
1. d4 e5 2. e4
```

Transposes to various e4 e5 lines. If you play the Englund, you probably don't want this!

**After 2...exd4**, you're in a Center Game/Danish Gambit complex.
**After 2...Nc6 3. d5**, you're in a Steinitz Defense (reversed).

---

## White Plays 2. d5

```
1. d4 e5 2. d5 Bc5
```

White closes the center and ignores the pawn offer.

**Black's setup:**
- Develop pieces: ...Nf6, ...0-0, ...d6
- Aim to challenge d5 with ...c6

**Evaluation:** Playable for both sides, but White's space advantage is real.

---

# PART 3: MOVE-BY-MOVE LINES (With Annotations)

## Line A: The Critical Main Line

```
1. d4 e5        (The gambit!)
2. dxe5 Nc6    (Developing and pressuring e5)
3. Nf3 Qe7    (Pinning the e5 pawn to the King; threatening Qb4+)
4. Bf4         (Best. Defends e5 solidly)
   ...Qb4+    (The "trap" begins)
5. Bd2 Qxb2   (Black goes for maximum greed)
6. Bc3!!       (The refutation!)
   ...Bb4     (Black tries to escape)
7. Qd2!        (Double attack on b4 and c3)
   ...Bxc3    (Forced)
8. Qxc3        (Threatening Qxb2)
   ...Qxa1    (Black takes the Rook, but...)
9. Qxg7!       (White attacks the Kingside savagely)
   ...Qxb1+   (Only move)
10. Kd2 Qxf1?? (Greedy and loses)
11. Qxh8+ Kd8 (Forced)
12. Qxh7       (White is up crushing material with attack)
```

**Final Evaluation:** White is winning. Black's pieces are uncoordinated, King is stuck in the center, and White has a massive attack.

---

## Line B: The Respected Main Line (Avoiding Traps)

```
1. d4 e5
2. dxe5 Nc6
3. Nf3 Qe7
4. Qd5!        (White's best! Centralizing and defending)
   ...f6       (Black must open lines)
5. exf6 Nxf6
6. Qd1 d5     (Black gets central pawns as compensation)
7. c3 Bf5     (Development with purpose)
8. Nbd2 0-0-0 (Black castles long for aggression)
```

**Evaluation:** White is slightly better (~+0.4), but Black has real practical chances. The position is sharp and double-edged.

**Black's Plan:**
- Attack on the d-file and Kingside
- Use the central pawn mass
- Create threats faster than White can consolidate

---

## Line C: The Hartlaub-Charlick (Solid Alternative)

```
1. d4 e5
2. dxe5 d6    (Immediately pressuring e5)
3. exd6 Bxd6  (Recapturing with development)
4. Nf3 Nf6
5. Nc3 0-0   (Quick development)
6. Bg5 h6
7. Bh4 Re8   (Seizing the open e-file)
8. e3 Nc6
9. Be2 Bf5   (Both bishops developed actively)
```

**Evaluation:** White is still slightly better (+0.5), but this is much more playable than the mainline traps. Black has:
- Full development
- Open files
- Active pieces
- Real counterplay

---

## Line D: The Zilbermints Gambit (Modern Approach)

```
1. d4 e5
2. dxe5 Nc6
3. Nf3 Nge7!?  (The Zilbermints move! Preparing ...Ng6)
4. Bg5 h6
5. Bh4 g5!?    (Aggressive! Trapping the Bishop)
6. Bg3 Bg7    (Fianchetto)
7. e3 Ng6      (Knight goes to a great square)
8. Nc3 Ncxe5  (Regaining the pawn)
9. Nxe5 Nxe5
```

**Evaluation:** Approximately equal. Black has successfully neutralized the gambit's drawbacks and has a playable position.

# PART 4: STRATEGIC THEMES

## Black's Compensation

When playing the Englund, Black seeks:

| Compensation | How to Achieve It |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **Rapid Development** | Don't waste time; every move counts |
| **Open Lines** | Use the e-file and diagonals |
| **Tactical Chances** | Look for forks, pins, and attacks on f2/g2 |
| **White's Unfamiliarity** | Most d4 players don't know the traps |
| **Time Pressure** | In blitz, Black's activity creates chaos |

---

## Typical Attacking Patterns

### The f2 Attack
```
After ...Bc5, ...Qb6, aim for Bxf2+ tricks
```

### The b2 Raid
```
...Qb4+ followed by ...Qxb2, threatening Ra1
```

### The g2 Weakness
```
After castling, Qxg2 threats or Qxg7 sacrifices
```

### The Pin on f3
```
...Bg4 pins the Knight; coordinate with Queen attacks
```

---

## White's Best Strategy

If you're facing the Englund:

1. **Take the pawn** (2. dxe5)
2. **Defend solidly** (Nf3, Bf4)
3. **Don't fall for Qxb2 traps** (Play Bc3!, not Nc3??)
4. **Centralize the Queen** (Qd5 is strong)
5. **Don't panic** — you're objectively better

# Pros & Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| **Surprise Value:** Almost nobody expects it | **Objectively Bad:** White is just better with correct play |
| **Traps:** Many players fall for the Qxb2 line | **Theory Risk:** If White knows Bc3!, you're suffering |
| **Fun:** Dynamic, attacking chess | **Reputation:** May not be taken seriously |
| **Blitz Weapon:** Chaos favors the prepared | **Limited Improvement:** Doesn't teach sound chess |

---

# When to Use (and When Not To)

| Situation | Englund? | Reasoning |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Blitz/Bullet** | ✅ Yes | Chaos and traps shine here |
| **Rapid (15+10)** | ⚠️ Maybe | More time for White to refute |
| **Classical (30+ min)** | ❌ No | White will find the correct play |
| **Must-Win** | ✅ Yes | Creates winning chances from chaos |
| **Serious Tournament** | ❌ No | Risk-reward ratio is poor |
| **For Fun** | ✅ Absolutely | That's the whole point! |
