## The Moves
**1. d4 d5 2. Bf4** (or 1. d4 Nf6 2. Bf4)

## The Concept
The London System is a "system" opening, meaning White aims for the same piece setup regardless of what Black plays. White develops the dark-squared bishop to f4 *before* playing e3, ensuring it isn't trapped inside the pawn chain (unlike the Queen's Gambit). White then builds a solid pyramid of pawns (c3, d4, e3) and develops pieces to natural squares (Nf3, Bd3, Nbd2). It is low-maintenance, extremely solid, and avoids memorizing deep theoretical lines.

> **Modern Revival:** Once considered boring, the London has been revitalized by top players like Magnus Carlsen and Gata Kamsky as a reliable weapon against any Black setup.

---

## Main Variations

### Classical London: `1. d4 d5 2. Bf4 Nf6 3. e3`
*The standard setup. White builds the pyramid and prepares for a slow squeeze.*

**ECO Codes:** D00

**Key Moves:**
- 3... c5 4. c3 Nc6 5. Nd2 e6 6. Ngf3 Bd6 (Black challenges the f4 bishop)
- 3... e6 4. Nd2 c5 5. c3 Nc6 6. Ngf3 (solid development)

---

### Jobava London: `1. d4 d5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. Bf4`
*A more aggressive, modern interpretation. White blocks the c-pawn with Nc3 to play for quick piece activity and attacks on the center/kingside.*

**ECO Codes:** D00

**Key Ideas:**
- Prepares e4 in one move
- More tactical than classical London
- Can lead to sharp positions

---

### London vs King's Indian: `1. d4 Nf6 2. Bf4 g6`
*Black fianchettoes the dark-squared bishop to g7. White usually plays h3 and Bh2 to preserve the bishop and attacks the kingside with h4-h5.*

**Key Moves:**
- 3. e3 Bg7 4. Nf3 0-0 5. Be2 d6 6. h3 (preparing Bh2 if ...Nh5)
- White often plays c3, Nbd2, and aims for e4

---

### London vs Grünfeld Setup: `1. d4 Nf6 2. Bf4 g6 3. e3 Bg7 4. Nf3 d5`
*Black plays ...d5 like a Grünfeld. White can continue with c3 and maintain the pyramid.*

## Pros & Cons

| Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Universal:** You can play it against almost anything (d5, Nf6, f5). | **Passive:** Can be too passive if White doesn't know how to attack later. |
| **Low Theory:** Understanding the plans is more important than move orders. | **Equality:** Black can equalize fairly easily with accurate play. |
| **Safety:** Very hard to lose quickly with the London. | **Repetitive:** You get similar pawn structures in almost every game. |

## 🏛️ Typical Pawn Structures

### The London Pyramid
```
White pawns: c3, d4, e3
Black pawns: varies (often c5, d5, e6 or g6)
```
*White's pyramid is extremely solid. The plan is to improve pieces (Bd3, Nbd2-f3) and then break with e4 or attack on the kingside.*

### After c4 (Aggressive London)
```
White pawns: c4, d4, e3
Black pawns: d5, e6 (or c5)
```
*White plays c4 instead of c3, transposing to QGD-like structures. More space but less of the "true" London spirit.*

### Closed Center
```
White pawns: c3, d4, e3, f4 (stonewall hybrid)
Black pawns: c5, d5, e6
```
*In some lines, White plays f4 to create a modified Stonewall Attack.*

## ⚔️ Middlegame Plans

### For White:
1. **The Pyramid:** Build c3-d4-e3, develop Nf3, Bd3, Nbd2, 0-0
2. **The e5 Outpost:** Plant a Knight on e5. With Bf4 supporting it, it's a thorn in Black's side
3. **Kingside Attack:** If Black castles short, Qe2 or Qf3, Ng5, and attack on h7
4. **Bh2 Retreat:** If Black plays ...Nh5 attacking the bishop, play Bg3-Bh2 to preserve it
5. **e4 Break:** The strategic goal is to play e4 (or f2-f3 then e4) to open the position

### For Black:
1. **Challenge Bf4:** Play ...Bd6 to offer an exchange (often good for Black if allowed)
2. **Break in the Center:** ...c5 or ...e5 to challenge White's setup
3. **Target the Bishop:** After ...Nh5, the Bf4 is annoyed (must retreat or trade)
4. **Play for ...e5:** Gain space and activity

## Key Ideas

- **The "Pyramid":** White's pawns on c3, d4, and e3 form an unbreakable wall.
- **The e5 Outpost:** White often plants a Knight on e5. Since the f4 bishop supports it, it's a thorn in Black's side.
- **Kingside Attack:** If Black castles Kingside, White often directs the light-squared bishop (on d3) and Queen (via f3 or h5) towards the King.
- **Bh2 Retreat:** A thematic idea to preserve the bishop when challenged by ...Nh5.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Playing e3 before Bf4:** The bishop gets trapped inside the pawn chain
2. **Passive Play:** Building the pyramid but never playing for e4 or attacking
3. **Trading the f4 Bishop Too Easily:** It's your key piece—don't give it up without compensation
4. **Ignoring Black's ...Nh5:** Always have a plan (Bg3-Bh2 or Be5)
