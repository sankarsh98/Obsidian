## The Moves
**1. d4 Nf6 2. c4 g6 3. Nc3 Bg7 4. e4 d6**

## The Concept
The King's Indian Defense (KID) is a "hypermodern" opening. Instead of fighting for the center immediately with pawns, Black allows White to build a massive pawn center (c4, d4, e4) with the intention of attacking it later. Black fianchettoes the dark-squared Bishop and castles early. The game often becomes a race: White attacks on the Queenside while Black launches a "do-or-die" assault on the White King on the Kingside. It is a favorite of aggressive players like Kasparov and Fischer.

> **Philosophy:** The KID embodies the hypermodern principle: "Don't occupy the center; attack it!" Black trades space for piece activity and dynamic chances.

---

## Main Variations

### The Classical Variation: `5. Nf3 0-0 6. Be2 e5`
*The main battleground. White develops naturally, and Black strikes back in the center with e5.*

**ECO Codes:** E90-E99

**Key Lines:**
- **Mar del Plata Attack:** 7. 0-0 Nc6 8. d5 Ne7 9. Ne1 (both sides race!)
- **Petrosian System:** 7. d5 (closes center early)
- **Gligoric System:** 7. Be3 (flexible, preparing Qd2)

---

### The Sämisch Variation: `5. f3`
*White solidifies the center with f3, preventing Ng4 and preparing a Kingside pawn storm (g4, h4). This often leads to very sharp positions.*

**ECO Codes:** E80-E89

**Key Lines:**
- **Main Line:** 5... 0-0 6. Be3 e5 7. d5 (opposite-side castling battle)
- **Byrne Variation:** 5... 0-0 6. Bg5 (aggressive pin)
- **Panno Variation:** 5... 0-0 6. Be3 Nc6 (flexible)

---

### The Fianchetto Variation: `5. Nf3 0-0 6. g3`
*White plays solidly, fianchettoing their own Bishop to neutralize Black's strong g7 Bishop. This tends to be more positional and less tactical.*

**ECO Codes:** E60-E69

**Key Lines:**
- **Main Line:** 6... Nc6 7. Bg2 e5 8. d5 Ne7
- **Yugoslav System:** 6... c5 (fighting for d4)

---

### The Four Pawns Attack: `5. f4`
*White grabs maximum space with e4, d4, c4, f4. High-risk, high-reward; one mistake and the center collapses.*

**ECO Codes:** E76-E79

---

### Anti-King's Indian Systems

| Name | Moves | Character |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **London vs KID** | 2. Bf4 | Avoids main lines entirely |
| **Averbakh Variation** | 5. Be2 0-0 6. Bg5 | Positional, pins the King's Knight |
| **Makogonov Variation** | 5. Nf3 0-0 6. h3 | Slow, positional buildup |

## Pros & Cons

| Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Winning Chances:** Excellent for black players who need a win and don't mind risk. | **Space Disadvantage:** Black often suffers from a lack of space for a long time. |
| **Tactical Complexity:** Creates chaotic positions where stronger tactical players thrive. | **Computer Dislike:** Engines often hate the KID because they value space, but humans find it hard to play for White. |
| **System Based:** You can play the initial KID setup against almost anything White plays (except e4). | **The "Bayonet":** White's attacks on the queenside can sometimes arrive faster than Black's mate threats. |

## 🏛️ Typical Pawn Structures

### Classical KID (Locked Center)
```
White pawns: c4, d5, e4, f2
Black pawns: c7, d6, e5, f7/f5
```
*Center locked with d5 vs e5. White plays on queenside (c5), Black on kingside (f5-f4-g5-h5).*

### Open Center
```
White pawns: c4, e4
Black pawns: d6, f7
```
*After ...exd4 Nxd4. More maneuvering, less attacking race.*

### Sämisch Structure (After f3)
```
White pawns: c4, d4/d5, e4, f3
Black pawns: d6, e5, f7
```
*White's f3 solidifies the center but can be a target. Often opposite-side castling.*

## ⚔️ Middlegame Plans

### For White:
1. **Bayonet Attack:** b4-c5, attack on the queenside before Black's kingside attack arrives
2. **Exchange on d4:** Play d5 to lock the center, then expand on the other wing
3. **Blockade the f4 Break:** Use pieces to prevent Black's ...f4 pawn storm
4. **Queenside Breakthrough:** c5 break, open the c-file, pressure d6

### For Black:
1. **The Kingside Pawn Storm:** f5, f4, g5, g4, h5 - sacrifice pawns to open lines towards the White King
2. **The f4 Wedge:** Once f4 is played, Ng6-Ne5 or Nf6-Nh5-Ng3 become powerful maneuvers
3. **The Mar del Plata Knight Maneuver:** Nf6-d7-f8-g6 (or h5) to reposition the Knight for the attack
4. **Exchange Sacrifice:** ...Rxf3 to rip open the kingside

## Key Ideas

- **The Kingside Pawn Storm:** In the Classical lines, Black often pushes f7-f5, then g5 and h5, engaging in a direct mating attack.
- **The Locked Center:** If White plays d5 and Black plays e5, the center becomes locked. This dictates the plans: White plays on the Queenside (c5 break), Black plays on the Kingside (f5 break).
- **The "Mar del Plata" Knight:** A thematic maneuver is Nf6-d7 (or e8), moving the knight out of the way of the f-pawn and preparing to recycle to f8 or c5.
- **The Bayonet Attack:** White's most dangerous weapon: b4-c5, trying to break through before Black mates.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes for Black

1. **Playing ...e5 Too Early:** Must be timed with proper development
2. **Slow Kingside Attack:** If you don't attack fast enough, White's queenside attack arrives first
3. **Bad ...f5 Timing:** Playing f5 before castling or before e5 is secured can backfire
4. **Ignoring Queenside:** While attacking, don't forget to slow down White's counterplay
