# 🧭 Opening Repertoire Picker

Not sure what to play? Use this guide to find your perfect opening based on your style, rating, and goals.

### Question 1: How do you handle conflict?

| Answer | Your Style | Go To |

| **B)** I build a fortress and wait for them to mess up | Solid | [[#🛡️ The Solid Path]] |

## 📊 Rating-Based Recommendations

### 🟢 Beginner (Under 1200)

**Focus on:** Learning the opening principles, not memorizing moves.

| As | Opening | Why? |
|----|---------|------|
| **White** | [[The Italian Game]] | Teaches basic development and tactics |
| **White** | [[The London System]] | Easy setup, works against anything |
| **Black vs 1. e4** | [[The Scandinavian Defense]] | Simple, forces the game open |
| **Black vs 1. d4** | [[The London System]] (reversed) | Mirror White's ideas |

### 🟡 Intermediate (1200-1800)

**Focus on:** Understanding pawn structures and typical plans.

| As | Opening | Why? |
|----|---------|------|
| **White** | [[The Ruy Lopez]] | Classical chess, teaches deep strategy |
| **White** | [[The Queens Gambit]] | Central control, positional themes |
| **Black vs 1. e4** | [[The Caro-Kann Defense]] | Solid, leads to good endgames |
| **Black vs 1. d4** | [[The Nimzo-Indian Defense]] or [[The Queens Gambit]] (Declined) | Reliable, flexible |

### 🔴 Advanced (1800+)

**Focus on:** Deep theoretical knowledge and concrete variations.

| As | Opening | Why? |
|----|---------|------|
| **White** | [[The Ruy Lopez]] + [[The English Opening]] | Full repertoire for e4/c4 |
| **White** | [[The Queens Gambit]] + [[The Catalan]] | Full repertoire for d4 |
| **Black vs 1. e4** | [[The Sicilian Defense]] (Najdorf or Sveshnikov) | Maximum winning chances |
| **Black vs 1. d4** | [[The Kings Indian Defense]] or [[The Nimzo-Indian Defense]] | Dynamic or strategic options |

## 🔥 The Aggressive Path
*You love tactics, sacrifices, and checkmating attacks.*

### As White (1. e4):
- **[[The Italian Game]]**: Specifically the *Evans Gambit* or *Fried Liver Attack*.
  - *Why:* Direct attacking chances, piece sacrifices, and mating attacks.
- **The Scotch Game**: Open the center immediately and attack.
  - *Moves:* 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. d4

### As White (1. d4):
- **The Jobava London**: An aggressive twist on the London.
  - *Moves:* 1. d4 d5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. Bf4
- **The Trompowsky**: Surprise attack on the Knight.
  - *Moves:* 1. d4 Nf6 2. Bg5

### As Black (vs 1. e4):
- **[[The Sicilian Defense]]**: The Najdorf or Dragon.
  - *Why:* Asymmetrical, fighting for a win, tactical chaos.

### As Black (vs 1. d4):
- **[[The Kings Indian Defense]]**: Let them take the center, then checkmate their King.
  - *Why:* Kingside pawn storms, attacking races, dynamic play.

## 🛡️ The Solid Path
*You prefer positional squeezes, endgames, and safety.*

### As White:
- **[[The London System]]**: Setup your pyramid and grind them down.
  - *Why:* Low maintenance, very safe, hard to lose quickly.
- **[[The Queens Gambit]]**: Classic, sound, and controlling.
  - *Why:* Central domination, clear plans, no gimmicks.

### As Black (vs 1. e4):
- **[[The Caro-Kann Defense]]**: The "Iron Wall."
  - *Why:* Extremely solid, solves the "French bishop problem."
- **The Petroff Defense**: Frustrate attackers with symmetry.
  - *Moves:* 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nf6

### As Black (vs 1. d4):
- **[[The Queens Gambit]] (Declined)**: Do not yield an inch.
  - *Why:* Rock-solid, leads to clear strategic play.
- **[[The Nimzo-Indian Defense]]**: Strategic complexity without high risk.
  - *Why:* Flexible, stops e4, creates imbalances safely.

---

## 🌀 The Chaos Path
*You like unbalanced positions where memorization matters less.*

### As White:
- **[[The English Opening]]**: Flank attacks and transpositions.
  - *Why:* Avoids main lines, flexible, opponents get confused.
- **Bird's Opening (1. f4)**: Weird Dutch setups as White.
  - *Moves:* 1. f4
- **King's Indian Attack**: A system that works against everything.
  - *Moves:* 1. Nf3, 2. g3, 3. Bg2, 4. d3, 5. e4, 6. Nbd2

### As Black (vs 1. e4):
- **[[The Scandinavian Defense]]**: Force the Queen out early and scrap.
  - *Why:* Forcing, avoids theory, leads to active piece play.
- **The Alekhine Defense**: 1... Nf6 provokes the pawns forward.
  - *Moves:* 1. e4 Nf6

### As Black (vs 1. d4):
- **[[The Nimzo-Indian Defense]]**: Pin their knights and ruin their pawns.
  - *Why:* Creates structural imbalances, active piece play.
- **The Dutch Defense**: 1... f5 grabs kingside space.
  - *Moves:* 1. d4 f5

## 📈 Study Priority Chart

> Start with the openings you'll face most often!

### As White:
1. First: Main responses to 1. e4 e5 or 1. d4 d5
2. Second: Sicilian Defense (if you play e4)
3. Third: Sidelines and surprise weapons

### As Black:
1. First: Response to 1. e4 (you'll face this most)
2. Second: Response to 1. d4
3. Third: Anti-system defenses (vs London, Colle, etc.)

## 🔄 Building a Complete Repertoire

### For White (e4 player):
```
1. e4 → Italian or Ruy Lopez (vs 1... e5)
     → Anti-Sicilian or Open Sicilian (vs 1... c5)  
     → Advance or Exchange (vs Caro-Kann, French)
```

### For White (d4 player):
```
1. d4 → Queen's Gambit (vs 1... d5)
     → Main lines or London (vs Indian setups)
```

### For Black:
```
vs 1. e4 → Choose ONE: Sicilian, Caro-Kann, French, or Scandinavian
vs 1. d4 → Choose ONE: King's Indian, Nimzo-Indian, or QGD
vs 1. c4/Nf3 → Transpose to your d4 system
```
