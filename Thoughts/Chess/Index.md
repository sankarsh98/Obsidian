## 📅 Study Plan

### Daily Routine (30-60 min)
- **Tactics (15 min):** Solve 10-20 puzzles on Lichess/Chess.com
- **Games (15 min):** Play 1-2 games with analysis
- **Opening (10 min):** Review one line from your repertoire

### Weekly Goals
- **Review 1 opening variation** from [[Chess/Openings/_Index|the Index]]
- **Study 1 master game** from the collection above
- **Practice 1 endgame pattern** (Lucena, Philidor, Opposition)

### Monthly Goals
- **Add a new opening** to your repertoire
- **Analyze 5 of your games** using the [[Chess/Templates/Game Analysis|template]]
- **Complete a tactical theme** (e.g., all "Pin" puzzles on Lichess)

## 📊 Progress by Rating Level

> Click your current level for a focused study plan

### Under 1200 (Beginner)
1. Learn [[Chess/Endgames/Endgame Fundamentals#♟️ Pawn Endings|King + Pawn vs King]]
2. Master [[Chess/Tactics/Common Tactical Motifs#🟢 Basic Motifs|Basic Tactics]]
3. Choose openings from [[Chess/Tools/Repertoire Picker#🟢 Beginner (Under 1200)|Beginner Repertoire]]

### 1200-1600 (Intermediate)
1. Study [[Chess/Endgames/Endgame Fundamentals#♜ Rook Endings|Rook Endgames]]
2. Learn [[Chess/Tactics/Common Tactical Motifs#🟡 Intermediate Motifs|Intermediate Tactics]]
3. Build a real repertoire with [[Chess/Openings/_Index|Openings Index]]

### 1600-2000 (Advanced)
1. Master all [[Chess/Endgames/Endgame Fundamentals|Endgame Types]]
2. Study [[Chess/Tactics/Common Tactical Motifs#🔴 Advanced Motifs|Advanced Patterns]]
3. Deep dive into your openings with move-by-move analysis

### 2000+ (Expert)
1. Analyze master games deeply
2. Prepare specific lines against strong opponents
3. Work on calculation and time management
