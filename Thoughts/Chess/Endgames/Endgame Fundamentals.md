# 🏁 Endgame Fundamentals

The endgame is where the advantage is converted into a win or a lost position is saved for a draw. Jose Raul Capablanca advised: *"In order to improve your game, you must study the endgame before everything else."*

## 👑 King Activity
In the opening and middlegame, the King is a baby that needs protection. In the endgame, the King is a **gladiator**.
*   **Rule:** Bring your King to the center immediately. It needs to support pawns and restrict the enemy King.

## ♟️ Pawn Promotion
The ultimate goal of most endgames.
*   **Passed Pawn:** A pawn with no opposing pawns in front of it or on adjacent files. It is a criminal; it must be handcuffed (blockaded) or it will become a Queen.
*   **Rule of the Square:** A visual trick to see if a King can catch a passed pawn. Draw a diagonal line from the pawn to the promotion rank to form a square. If the King is in the square, he catches it.

## 🤝 Opposition
A critical concept in King vs. King + Pawn endings.
*   **Definition:** When the Kings face each other with one square in between. The player who *does not* have the move has the "opposition" (advantage).
*   **Goal:** Use opposition to shoulder-barge the enemy King away and clear a path for your pawn.

## 🧱 Rook Endings
The most common type of endgame.
*   **Philidor Position (The Draw):** The defending King is on the promotion square. The defending Rook stays on the 3rd rank (preventing the enemy King from approaching), then checks from behind once the pawn advances.
*   **Lucena Position (The Win):** The attacking King is in front of the pawn (7th rank). The "Bridge Building" technique is used to shield the King from checks using the Rook.

## ♝ Minor Piece Endings
*   **Bishops:**
    *   *Opposite Colored Bishops:* notoriously drawish, even down 1 or 2 pawns, because one player controls squares the other can never touch.
    *   *Same Colored Bishops:* The player with better pawns/king usually wins.
*   **Knights:**
    *   Knights are terrible at stopping outside passed pawns (they are slow).
    *   Knight endgames play like Pawn endgames (pure calculation).

## 🏰 Fortress
A defensive setup where the weaker side cannot be breached, even with a material disadvantage.
*   **Example:** Wrong colored bishop pawn. If you have an h-pawn and a bishop that doesn't control the h8 promotion square, the enemy King just sits on h8 and draws.
