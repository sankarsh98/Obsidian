# Ashtakavarga (Eight-Fold Classification)

## Introduction

**Ashtakavarga** (अष्टकवर्ग) is a sophisticated mathematical system in Vedic Astrology used to quantify the strength of planets and houses. The term comes from *Ashta* (Eight) and *Varga* (Division/Group). It refers to the eight sources of energy that influence a planet: the seven planets (Sun to Saturn) and the Ascendant (Lagna).

Unlike the interpretative complexity of Yogas or Aspects, Ashtakavarga provides a clear **numerical score** (Bindus or Rekhas) to assess prediction. It is famously praised by Sage Parashara as the "lamp that illuminates the darkness" for astrologers in the Kali Yuga (current age), where human intellect is limited.

## The Concept of Bindus (Points)

In this system, benefic points are called **Bindus** (dots) or sometimes **Rekhas** (lines), depending on the regional tradition (North vs. South India).
*   *Common Convention:* High points are Good; Low points are Bad.
*   **Maximum Points per Sign:** 8 (contributed by 7 planets + Lagna).
*   **Minimum Points per Sign:** 0.

## Components of Ashtakavarga

### 1. Bhinnashtakavarga (Individual Planet's Score)

Every planet (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) releases benefic points into specific houses from its own position and from other planets' positions. This creates a separate chart for each planet called the **Bhinnashtakavarga**.

**Scoring Range:** 0 to 8 points per sign.
*   **0-3 Points:** Weak/Malefic. The planet gives little to no good results in this sign.
*   **4 Points:** Average/Neutral.
*   **5-8 Points:** Strong/Benefic. The planet gives excellent results during transit through this sign.

### 2. Sarvashtakavarga (Total Score)

This is the **Summary Chart** created by summing up the points of all 7 planets in each sign. (Note: Rahu and Ketu do not have Ashtakavarga).

**Total Points in Chart:** 337.
**Average Points per House:** ~28.

**Interpretation of House Scores:**
*   **> 30 Points:** Very Strong House. Events related to this house flourish.
*   **25 - 30 Points:** Average House. Mixed results.
*   **< 25 Points:** Weak House. Struggle and decay in these areas.

**Example:**
*   **11th House (Gains) has 40 points:** The person gains wealth easily with little effort.
*   **11th House has 18 points:** The person struggles to save or earn despite hard work.

## Calculating Contribution (Standard Rules)

Each planet contributes points to signs located at specific distances from itself and other planets.

*Example (Sun's Contribution):*
The Sun gives a point in the 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, and 11th houses from itself. It also gives points based on positions from Mars, Moon, etc.

*(Full calculation tables are extensive and usually handled by software, but the interpretation is manual).*

## Application of Ashtakavarga

### 1. Assessing Transit Strength (Gochar)

This is the most popular use. A transit's effect depends heavily on the Bindus in the sign being transited.

*   **Saturn Transit:**
    *   If Saturn transits a sign with **0-1 Bindu** in Saturn's Bhinnashtakavarga: **Disastrous**. Severe sorrow, loss, death-like suffering.
    *   If Saturn transits a sign with **6-7 Bindus**: **Excellent**. Massive success, structure, and gains.
    *   *Note:* This explains why Sade Sati is ruinous for some but creates leaders of others.

*   **Jupiter Transit:**
    *   Needs high points (5+) in Jupiter's BAV (Bhinnashtakavarga) to deliver blessings.

### 2. Assessing House Strength

*   **Longevity (Ayurdaya):** Strong 8th house points + Strong Saturn points.
*   **Wealth:** Strong 2nd (Wealth) and 11th (Gains) house points. Ideally, 11th > 10th > 12th (Income > Work > Expense).
    *   *Wealth Yoga:* If 11th house points > 12th house points, the person accumulates wealth. If 12th > 11th, they spend more than they earn.
*   **Career:** Strong 10th house points (SAV) and Sun/Saturn points.

### 3. Kakshya (Orbital Division)

Each sign (30°) is divided into 8 parts (Kakshyas) of 3°45' each. Each part is ruled by a planet (Saturn to Lagna).
*   **Usage:** Pinpointing the exact day/week of an event.
*   Even if a sign has low points, if a planet transits a Kakshya ruled by a planet that contributed a point (Bindu), that specific period will be good.

### 4. Special Determinations

*   **Direction of Success:** The cardinal direction represented by the sign with the highest points often indicates the direction where the person will find success (e.g., Aries = East, Libra = West).
*   **Timing Life Periods:** Shodhya Pinda calculations (reducing the scores to find remainder values) are used to time marriage, death, and childbirth.

## Guidelines for Interpretation

1.  **Look at the Totals (SAV):**
    *   Identify the strongest houses (>30). These are the pillars of the person's life.
    *   Identify the weakest houses (<25). These areas require [[Remedies]].
2.  **Compare Pairs:**
    *   **1st vs 7th:** Self vs. Partner. High 1st/Low 7th = Domineering self. Low 1st/High 7th = Submissive to partner.
    *   **10th vs 11th:** Effort vs. Gain. High 10th/Low 11th = Hard work, low pay. Low 10th/High 11th = Easy money/Luck.
3.  **Check Transits:**
    *   Before predicting doom during a bad transit, check the bindus. High bindus provide a shield.

## Common Myths

*   **"High points mean everything is perfect":** No. If the Dasha is bad, high points only minimize the damage. They cannot create an event without Dasha support.
*   **"Low points mean failure":** No. It means *struggle*. One can still succeed with low points (e.g., in the 10th house) through immense sheer willpower, but it won't come naturally.
