# Planetary Strengths (Shadbala)

## Introduction

**Shadbala** (षड्बल) literally means "Six Strengths" (Shad = Six, Bala = Strength). It is a complex mathematical model described in **Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra** to quantify the exact strength of each planet.

While a planet may appear strong by sign (e.g., Exalted), it might be weak in other ways (e.g., Retrograde, winning a planetary war, or in a bad time). Shadbala provides the **net resultant strength** in Rupas (units).

**Importance:**
*   Determines which planet will dominate in a chart.
*   Crucial for resolving conflicts (e.g., two planets aspecting the same house—who wins?).
*   Essential for prediction of Dasha results.

## The Six Sources of Strength

### 1. Sthana Bala (Positional Strength)
Strength derived from the planet's standing position in the zodiac.
*   **Uccha Bala:** Exaltation strength.
*   **Saptavarga Bala:** Strength in 7 divisional charts.
*   **Ojhayugmarasyamsa Bala:** Strength from odd/even signs in Rasi and Navamsa.
*   **Kendra Bala:** Strength from being in Angular houses (1, 4, 7, 10).
*   **Drekkana Bala:** Strength based on placement in masculine/feminine/neuter decanates.

### 2. Dig Bala (Directional Strength)
Strength derived from the direction the planet rules.
*   **East (Ascendant):** Mercury and Jupiter are strongest here.
*   **South (10th House):** Sun and Mars are strongest here (High Noon).
*   **West (7th House):** Saturn is strongest here (Sunset).
*   **North (4th House):** Moon and Venus are strongest here (Midnight).

### 3. Kaala Bala (Temporal Strength)
Strength derived from Time.
*   **Natonnata Bala:** Day vs. Night strength.
    *   Sun, Jupiter, Venus strong during Day.
    *   Moon, Mars, Saturn strong during Night.
    *   Mercury strong at both times (twilight).
*   **Paksha Bala:** Lunar Phase strength (Waxing vs. Waning).
*   **Tribhaga Bala:** Strength based on the 1/3rd portion of the day/night of birth.
*   **Varsha/Masa/Dina/Hora Bala:** Lord of the Year, Month, Day, and Hour get points.
*   **Ayanabala:** Equinoctial strength (North/South declination).
*   **Yuddha Bala:** Planetary War. If two planets are within 1°, the victor gets the loser's points.

### 4. Chesta Bala (Motional Strength)
Strength derived from movement.
*   **Vakra (Retrograde):** Retrograde planets get high Chesta Bala.
*   **Anuvakra:** Entering the previous sign in retrograde.
*   **Vikala:** Stationary.
*   **Mandatara:** Slowing down.
*   *Note:* Sun and Moon do not have Chesta Bala (they never go retrograde), so their Ayana Bala is used instead.

### 5. Naisargika Bala (Natural Strength)
A fixed value assigned to each planet based on its natural luminosity.
1.  Sun (Strongest)
2.  Moon
3.  Venus
4.  Jupiter
5.  Mercury
6.  Mars
7.  Saturn (Weakest)

*Interpretation:* Naturally, the Sun is more powerful than Saturn. Saturn needs other strengths (Position, Time) to overpower the Sun.

### 6. Drik Bala (Aspectual Strength)
Strength gained or lost through aspects from other planets.
*   **Benefic Aspects (Jupiter, Venus):** Add strength.
*   **Malefic Aspects (Saturn, Mars):** Subtract strength.

## Ishta Phala and Kashta Phala

From Shadbala, two derived values are calculated:
*   **Ishta Phala:** The "Good Fruit" or positive potential.
*   **Kashta Phala:** The "Bad Fruit" or negative potential.

*Example:* A planet may have high Shadbala (Total Strength) but high Kashta Phala. This means it is a "Strong Malefic" — it has the power to do harm efficiently.

## Minimum Requirements (Rupas)

To be considered effective, planets must meet these minimum Shadbala scores (in Rupas):

| Planet | Minimum Strength |
|--------|------------------|
| Sun | 6.5 |
| Moon | 6.0 |
| Mars | 5.0 |
| Mercury| 7.0 |
| Jupiter| 6.5 |
| Venus | 5.5 |
| Saturn | 5.0 |

## Bhava Bala (House Strength)

Just as planets have strength, Houses (Bhavas) have **Bhava Bala**.
It combines:
1.  **Bhavadhipati Bala:** Strength of the House Lord (Shadbala).
2.  **Bhava Dig Bala:** Directional strength of the house signs.
3.  **Bhava Drishti Bala:** Aspects on the house.

**Usage:** If the 10th House has high Bhava Bala, career matters will be prominent in life, regardless of whether the career is "good" or "bad" (which depends on planetary nature).
