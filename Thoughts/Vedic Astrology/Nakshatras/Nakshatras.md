# Nakshatras

**Nakshatras** (Sanskrit: नक्षत्र, "star" or "lunar mansion") are the 27 (or 28) divisions of the zodiac in Vedic Astrology, each spanning 13°20' of arc. They represent the Moon's daily journey through the sky and provide profound insights into an individual's psychological makeup, destiny, and karmic patterns.

## Understanding Nakshatras

While [[Zodiac Signs (Rashis)]] divide the sky into 12 parts of 30° each, Nakshatras provide a finer, more detailed division. The Moon spends approximately one day in each Nakshatra, completing the cycle in about 27 days.

The Nakshatra occupied by the Moon at birth is particularly significant, determining:
- Birth [[Planetary Periods (Dashas)|Dasha]] (planetary period)
- Mental and emotional nature
- Karmic patterns and life purpose
- Marriage compatibility (Nakshatra matching)
- Name selection for newborns
- Muhurta (electional astrology) timing

## The 27 Nakshatras

### 1. Ashwini (0°00' - 13°20' Aries)
**Deity**: Ashwini Kumaras (divine physicians)
**Symbol**: Horse's head
**Ruling Planet**: Ketu
**Nature**: Swift, pioneering, healing
**Qualities**: Initiative, speed, healing abilities

### 2. Bharani (13°20' - 26°40' Aries)
**Deity**: Yama (god of death)
**Symbol**: Yoni (female reproductive organ)
**Ruling Planet**: Venus
**Nature**: Creative, restraining, transformative
**Qualities**: Creativity, responsibility, carrying burdens

### 3. Krittika (26°40' Aries - 10°00' Taurus)
**Deity**: Agni (fire god)
**Symbol**: Razor, flame
**Ruling Planet**: Sun
**Nature**: Sharp, cutting, purifying
**Qualities**: Determination, sharp intellect, leadership

### 4. Rohini (10°00' - 23°20' Taurus)
**Deity**: Brahma (creator god)
**Symbol**: Ox cart, chariot
**Ruling Planet**: Moon
**Nature**: Growth, beauty, materialistic
**Qualities**: Charm, creativity, material prosperity

### 5. Mrigashira (23°20' Taurus - 6°40' Gemini)
**Deity**: Soma (moon god)
**Symbol**: Deer's head
**Ruling Planet**: Mars
**Nature**: Gentle, searching, restless
**Qualities**: Curiosity, research, seeking

### 6. Ardra (6°40' - 20°00' Gemini)
**Deity**: Rudra (storm god)
**Symbol**: Teardrop, diamond
**Ruling Planet**: Rahu
**Nature**: Fierce, transformative, intellectual
**Qualities**: Intelligence, destruction-creation, intensity

### 7. Punarvasu (20°00' Gemini - 3°20' Cancer)
**Deity**: Aditi (mother of gods)
**Symbol**: Bow and quiver
**Ruling Planet**: Jupiter
**Nature**: Renewable, optimistic, philosophical
**Qualities**: Return, renewal, spirituality

### 8. Pushya (3°20' - 16°40' Cancer)
**Deity**: Brihaspati (priest of gods)
**Symbol**: Udder of cow, lotus
**Ruling Planet**: Saturn
**Nature**: Nourishing, auspicious, spiritual
**Qualities**: Nourishment, wisdom, devotion

### 9. Ashlesha (16°40' - 30°00' Cancer)
**Deity**: Nagas (serpent deities)
**Symbol**: Coiled serpent
**Ruling Planet**: Mercury
**Nature**: Cunning, mysterious, penetrating
**Qualities**: Wisdom, kundalini energy, mysticism

### 10. Magha (0°00' - 13°20' Leo)
**Deity**: Pitris (ancestors)
**Symbol**: Royal throne
**Ruling Planet**: Ketu
**Nature**: Majestic, traditional, ancestral
**Qualities**: Authority, respect for tradition, regality

### 11. Purva Phalguni (13°20' - 26°40' Leo)
**Deity**: Bhaga (god of fortune)
**Symbol**: Front legs of bed, hammock
**Ruling Planet**: Venus
**Nature**: Pleasurable, creative, relaxing
**Qualities**: Luxury, creativity, relationships

### 12. Uttara Phalguni (26°40' Leo - 10°00' Virgo)
**Deity**: Aryaman (god of contracts)
**Symbol**: Back legs of bed
**Ruling Planet**: Sun
**Nature**: Patronage, friendship, partnership
**Qualities**: Generosity, helping others, alliances

### 13. Hasta (10°00' - 23°20' Virgo)
**Deity**: Savitar (sun god of creativity)
**Symbol**: Hand, fist
**Ruling Planet**: Moon
**Nature**: Skillful, dexterous, craftsmanship
**Qualities**: Manual skill, resourcefulness, cleverness

### 14. Chitra (23°20' Virgo - 6°40' Libra)
**Deity**: Tvashtar (celestial architect)
**Symbol**: Bright jewel, pearl
**Ruling Planet**: Mars
**Nature**: Artistic, brilliant, creating
**Qualities**: Beauty, artistry, charisma

### 15. Swati (6°40' - 20°00' Libra)
**Deity**: Vayu (wind god)
**Symbol**: Young plant shoot
**Ruling Planet**: Rahu
**Nature**: Independent, flexible, dispersing
**Qualities**: Independence, flexibility, commerce

### 16. Vishakha (20°00' Libra - 3°20' Scorpio)
**Deity**: Indra-Agni (king of gods and fire)
**Symbol**: Triumphal arch, potter's wheel
**Ruling Planet**: Jupiter
**Nature**: Goal-oriented, determined, forked
**Qualities**: Ambition, determination, goal achievement

### 17. Anuradha (3°20' - 16°40' Scorpio)
**Deity**: Mitra (god of friendship)
**Symbol**: Lotus flower
**Ruling Planet**: Saturn
**Nature**: Devotional, friendly, success through struggle
**Qualities**: Devotion, discipline, friendship

### 18. Jyeshtha (16°40' - 30°00' Scorpio)
**Deity**: Indra (king of gods)
**Symbol**: Circular amulet, earring
**Ruling Planet**: Mercury
**Nature**: Protective, senior, responsible
**Qualities**: Authority, protection, seniority

### 19. Mula (0°00' - 13°20' Sagittarius)
**Deity**: Nirriti (goddess of destruction)
**Symbol**: Bunch of roots
**Ruling Planet**: Ketu
**Nature**: Exploring roots, destructive-creative
**Qualities**: Investigation, foundation, transformation

### 20. Purva Ashadha (13°20' - 26°40' Sagittarius)
**Deity**: Apas (water goddess)
**Symbol**: Elephant's tusk, fan
**Ruling Planet**: Venus
**Nature**: Invincible, purifying, victorious
**Qualities**: Invincibility, purification, optimism

### 21. Uttara Ashadha (26°40' Sagittarius - 10°00' Capricorn)
**Deity**: Vishvadevas (universal gods)
**Symbol**: Elephant's tusk
**Ruling Planet**: Sun
**Nature**: Final victory, universal, permanent
**Qualities**: Leadership, achievement, permanence

### 22. Shravana (10°00' - 23°20' Capricorn)
**Deity**: Vishnu (preserver god)
**Symbol**: Ear, three footprints
**Ruling Planet**: Moon
**Nature**: Listening, learning, connection
**Qualities**: Learning, knowledge, connection

### 23. Dhanishtha (23°20' Capricorn - 6°40' Aquarius)
**Deity**: Vasus (eight elemental gods)
**Symbol**: Drum, flute
**Ruling Planet**: Mars
**Nature**: Wealthy, musical, adaptable
**Qualities**: Wealth, music, versatility

### 24. Shatabhisha (6°40' - 20°00' Aquarius)
**Deity**: Varuna (god of cosmic waters)
**Symbol**: Empty circle, hundred flowers/healers
**Ruling Planet**: Rahu
**Nature**: Healing, secretive, mystical
**Qualities**: Healing, mystery, solitude

### 25. Purva Bhadrapada (20°00' Aquarius - 3°20' Pisces)
**Deity**: Aja Ekapada (one-footed goat)
**Symbol**: Front legs of funeral cot, sword
**Ruling Planet**: Jupiter
**Nature**: Fierce, transformative, dualistic
**Qualities**: Intensity, transformation, idealism

### 26. Uttara Bhadrapada (3°20' - 16°40' Pisces)
**Deity**: Ahir Budhnya (serpent of the deep)
**Symbol**: Back legs of funeral cot
**Ruling Planet**: Saturn
**Nature**: Deep, wise, mystical
**Qualities**: Depth, wisdom, kundalini power

### 27. Revati (16°40' - 30°00' Pisces)
**Deity**: Pushan (nourisher, protector)
**Symbol**: Fish, drum
**Ruling Planet**: Mercury
**Nature**: Prosperous, nourishing, safe journey
**Qualities**: Wealth, protection, journey completion

### 28. Abhijit (Optional Nakshatra)
Some traditions include a 28th Nakshatra called Abhijit, spanning a small portion of Uttara Ashadha and Shravana, ruled by Brahma and particularly auspicious.

## Nakshatra Padas (Quarters)

Each Nakshatra is divided into 4 padas (quarters) of 3°20' each, corresponding to the 12 zodiac signs cycling through. These padas provide additional nuance and are used in:
- [[Divisional Charts]] (especially Navamsa D-9)
- Character analysis
- Name selection

## Nakshatra Classifications

### Ganas (Temperament)
- **Deva (Divine)**: Ashwini, Mrigashira, Punarvasu, Pushya, Hasta, Swati, Anuradha, Shravana, Revati
- **Manushya (Human)**: Bharani, Rohini, Ardra, Purva Phalguni, Uttara Phalguni, Purva Ashadha, Uttara Ashadha, Purva Bhadrapada, Uttara Bhadrapada
- **Rakshasa (Demonic)**: Krittika, Ashlesha, Magha, Chitra, Vishakha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Dhanishtha, Shatabhisha

### Yoni (Animal Nature)
Each Nakshatra has an associated animal representing its primal nature, used in compatibility matching.

### Gunas (Qualities)
- Sattva (purity)
- Rajas (activity)
- Tamas (inertia)

## Importance in Vedic Astrology

Nakshatras are essential for:

1. **Dasha Calculation**: The [[Planetary Periods (Dashas)|Vimshottari Dasha]] system begins based on the Moon's Nakshatra at birth
2. **Marriage Matching**: Nakshatra compatibility (Ashta Koota system) is crucial for arranged marriages
3. **Character Analysis**: Provides deeper psychological insights than Sun or Moon signs alone
4. **Muhurta (Electional Astrology)**: Selecting auspicious times based on Nakshatra transits
5. **Naming**: Traditional Vedic naming uses specific sounds associated with each Nakshatra pada
6. **Medical Astrology**: Different Nakshatras govern different body parts and susceptibilities

The Moon's Nakshatra at birth, called the Janma Nakshatra, is considered one of the most personal and significant points in the [[Birth Chart (Kundali)]].
