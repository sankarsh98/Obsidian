# Houses (Bhavas)

In Vedic Astrology, **Bhavas** (Sanskrit: भाव, "state of being") or Houses represent 12 different life areas and experiences. The houses form the framework through which planetary energies manifest in specific domains of life. Starting from the Ascendant (Lagna), each house governs particular aspects of existence.

## The 12 Houses

### 1st House - Tanu Bhava (House of Self)

**Significations**:
- Physical body, appearance, health
- Personality, temperament, self-identity
- General life path and vitality
- Head and brain

**Karaka (Significator)**: Sun
**Natural Sign**: Aries

The 1st house is the most important house, representing the self and how one navigates life. A strong 1st house grants good health, confidence, and success.

### 2nd House - Dhana Bhava (House of Wealth)

**Significations**:
- Accumulated wealth, possessions
- Speech, voice, communication style
- Family (especially in early life)
- Food, eating habits
- Right eye, face, throat

**Karaka**: Jupiter (for wealth), Venus (for speech/face)
**Natural Sign**: Taurus

The 2nd house governs material resources and how one communicates and sustains themselves.

### 3rd House - Sahaj Bhava (House of Courage)

**Significations**:
- Siblings (especially younger)
- Courage, initiative, efforts
- Short journeys, neighbors
- Communication, writing, media
- Hobbies, skills, hands
- Right ear, shoulders, arms

**Karaka**: Mars
**Natural Sign**: Gemini

The 3rd house represents self-effort, bravery, and all forms of communication.

### 4th House - Sukha Bhava (House of Happiness)

**Significations**:
- Mother, maternal figures
- Home, property, vehicles
- Emotional security, inner peace
- Education (formal schooling)
- Heart, chest, breasts

**Karaka**: Moon (for mother), Venus/Mercury (for vehicles)
**Natural Sign**: Cancer

The 4th house governs emotional foundations, domestic life, and one's sense of belonging.

### 5th House - Putra Bhava (House of Children)

**Significations**:
- Children, creativity
- Intelligence, wisdom, discrimination
- Romance, love affairs
- Speculation, investments
- Past life merit (Purva Punya)
- Stomach, upper abdomen

**Karaka**: Jupiter
**Natural Sign**: Leo

The 5th house represents creative self-expression, intelligence, and progeny.

### 6th House - Ripu Bhava (House of Enemies)

**Significations**:
- Diseases, health issues
- Enemies, competitors, obstacles
- Debts, litigation
- Service, daily work routine
- Maternal uncle
- Digestive system, intestines

**Karaka**: Mars, Saturn
**Natural Sign**: Virgo

A dusthana (difficult house), the 6th represents challenges that build character through overcoming difficulties.

### 7th House - Kalatra Bhava (House of Partnership)

**Significations**:
- Spouse, marriage, partnerships
- Business partnerships
- Public dealings, contracts
- Sexual organs, lower abdomen

**Karaka**: Venus (for spouse in male chart), Jupiter (for spouse in female chart)
**Natural Sign**: Libra

The 7th house governs all one-to-one relationships and how one relates to others.

### 8th House - Ayu Bhava (House of Longevity)

**Significations**:
- Longevity, death, transformation
- Hidden matters, occult, mysticism
- Inheritance, unearned wealth
- Chronic diseases, accidents
- Sexual energy (Kundalini)
- Reproductive organs

**Karaka**: Saturn
**Natural Sign**: Scorpio

A dusthana house, the 8th represents transformation, mysteries, and what lies beneath the surface.

### 9th House - Dharma Bhava (House of Dharma)

**Significations**:
- Father, teachers, gurus
- Fortune, luck, providence
- Higher education, philosophy
- Long journeys, foreign travels
- Spirituality, religion, dharma
- Hips, thighs

**Karaka**: Jupiter, Sun (for father)
**Natural Sign**: Sagittarius

The most auspicious trikona (trine) house, representing higher purpose and divine grace.

### 10th House - Karma Bhava (House of Action)

**Significations**:
- Career, profession, vocation
- Social status, reputation
- Public life, authority
- Actions, deeds (karma)
- Knees

**Karaka**: Sun, Mercury, Saturn, Jupiter (depending on profession type)
**Natural Sign**: Capricorn

The most important kendra (angular house) for career and social standing.

### 11th House - Labha Bhava (House of Gains)

**Significations**:
- Income, gains, profits
- Friends, social networks
- Elder siblings
- Fulfillment of desires
- Left ear, calves, ankles

**Karaka**: Jupiter
**Natural Sign**: Aquarius

The house of acquisition and realization of ambitions through social connections.

### 12th House - Vyaya Bhava (House of Loss)

**Significations**:
- Losses, expenses, expenditures
- Foreign lands, isolation
- Spirituality, liberation (moksha)
- Sleep, bed pleasures
- Hospitals, prisons, ashrams
- Feet, left eye

**Karaka**: Saturn, Ketu
**Natural Sign**: Pisces

A dusthana house that represents endings, transcendence, and spiritual liberation.

## House Classifications

### Kendra (Angular Houses) - 1, 4, 7, 10
Most powerful houses providing stability and strength. Planets here give strong, tangible results.

### Trikona (Trinal Houses) - 1, 5, 9
Most auspicious houses representing dharma, fortune, and creativity.

### Dusthana (Difficult Houses) - 6, 8, 12
Challenging houses representing obstacles, transformation, and loss. Malefic planets do better here.

### Upachaya (Growing Houses) - 3, 6, 10, 11
Houses that improve over time. Malefic planets strengthen these areas.

### Maraka (Death-inflicting Houses) - 2, 7
Houses that can cause health issues or death during their [[Planetary Periods (Dashas)]].

## House Lordship

Each house is ruled by the planet that governs the [[Zodiac Signs (Rashis)|sign]] occupying that house. The house lord's placement shows where the results of that house will manifest.

For example, if Aries rises (Aries Ascendant):
- 1st house lord: Mars (owns Aries)
- 2nd house lord: Venus (owns Taurus)
- 3rd house lord: Mercury (owns Gemini)
... and so on.

## Using House Knowledge

Understanding houses is essential for:

- Interpreting the [[Birth Chart (Kundali)]]
- Analyzing where planetary energies manifest
- Understanding life timing through [[Planetary Periods (Dashas)]]
- Identifying [[Yogas]] formed by house lordships
- Prescribing [[Remedies]] for specific life areas

The interaction between [[Planets (Grahas)]], [[Zodiac Signs (Rashis)]], and Houses creates the complete astrological picture of an individual's life.
